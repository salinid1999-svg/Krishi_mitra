UIP Quick Facts

  • 🌱 BCG: Given at birth or as early as possible before 1 year. Protects against Tuberculosis.
  • 💧 OPV: Oral Polio Vaccine drops. Given at birth, 6, 10, and 14 weeks.
  • 🧬 Pentavalent: Protects against Diphtheria, Pertussis, Tetanus, Hepatitis B, and Hib. Given at 6, 10, and 14 weeks.
  • 🌀 Rotavirus: Given at 6, 10, and 14 weeks. Prevents severe diarrhea in infants.
  • 🍎 MR: Measles & Rubella. First dose at 9-12 months, second at 16-24 months.

National Health Protection Schemes

Ayushman Bharat - Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PM-JAY)

GOVT OF INDIA

World's largest government-funded healthcare program. Provides a cover of ₹5,00,000 per family per year for secondary and tertiary care hospitalization to over 12 crore poor and vulnerable families.

👍 Cashless hospitalization at all empanelled hospitals
🩺 Pre-existing diseases covered from day one
💊 Covers up to 3 days of pre-hospitalization & 15 days post-hospitalization

Pradhan Mantri Bhartiya Janaushadhi Pariyojana (PMBJP)

GOVT OF INDIA

Campaign launched by the Department of Pharmaceuticals to provide quality medicines at affordable prices to all through special Kendra outlets. Generic medicines are available at 50% to 90% cheaper rates than branded counterparts.

💊 Over 1800+ high-quality generic drugs and 280+ surgical items
🏪 Available across 10,000+ Jan Aushadhi Kendras in India
🔬 Standardized lab testing for biological efficacy before release

Critical Warnings & Early Intervention Guide

1. Cardiovascular Emergency vs Acidity

In India, many heart attacks are self-diagnosed as "gas" or "acidity", leading to fatal delays in treatment. Here are key warning signals:

Feature Acidity Cardiac Emergency
Pain Location Upper stomach, moving up to throat Center of chest, radiating to left arm, neck, jaw or back
Type of Discomfort Burning, worsens when lying flat Heavy squeezing pressure, tightness, feeling like a heavy weight
Associated Symptoms Sour burps, relived by antacids Profuse cold sweating, vomiting, dizziness, shortness of breath

2. Stroke Warning - Act F.A.S.T.

If a person shows signs of stroke, brain cells are dying every second. Check these points:

F - Face
Ask them to smile. Does one side of the face droop?
A - Arms
Ask them to raise both arms. Does one drift downward?
S - Speech
Ask them to repeat a simple phrase. Is their speech slurred?
T - Time
If any signs are positive, note the time and call 108 immediately.
